**The one big finding (P0):** the live EA is running a **mechanically different exit engine than
the one that passed the walk-forward/DSR ship gate.** The EA manages the lock/trail **per tick**;
the validated harness manages **on M15 bar close**. This is not a nuance: **8 of the 15 exits
(all the BE-scratches and fast trail-outs) occurred with ZERO bar-closes elapsed — an exit class
with probability 0 in the validated engine.** The +0.064R live aggregate landing near the +0.049R
backtest is (partly) coincidence of a 15-trade sample, not evidence of fidelity.

Everything else is working: entries fire on genuine ≥2 ATR impulses, the pullback limit sits
~0.6 ATR back, fills are clean, the spread gate holds live cost at/below model, sizing is correct
(0.28–0.59% risk incl. min-lot rounding), and churn/slippage fears did not materialize.

## 1. The P0 divergence, precisely (verified with line cites)

**Live EA (`mql5/DerivScalperEA.mq5`):**
- `ManageAll()` runs in `OnTick` on **every tick**, before the once-per-bar scan gate (:254-261).
- Lock arms the instant **intratick** profit ≥ 0.25·ATR (`profit = bid - entry`, :615, :619) and
  moves SL to **entry + (spread+2)pts** (:609) — a small *positive* lock.
- Trail then follows **live bid/ask** continuously at 0.5·ATR (:624), ratchet-only.
- ATR is **re-read every tick** from the last closed bar (:666) → management distances drift.

**Validated harness (`backtest/scalper_confluence.py`, the engine that earned SHIP):**
- Per M15 bar: SL checked against the bar's **range** first (stop-first pessimism, :345), TP second;
  only if the bar survives does lock/trail update **from the bar CLOSE** (:350-353), and the new SL
  applies to the **next** bar. Lock is to **exact entry** (0 gross). ATR is **frozen at the signal bar**.

**Mechanical consequences observed live (all four signatures verified):**
1. **Scratch swarm:** 7 trades exited +0.03..+0.06R within 2.5–11 minutes (MFE 0.35–0.55R). A
   momentary 0.25-ATR tick-spike arms the lock; the next wiggle tags BE+buffer. The validated
   engine required the *bar to close* ≥ +0.25 ATR — most of these trades would still be open
   with the full stop, en route to TP or −1R.
2. **Winners cut at ~1R:** trail-outs at +0.42..+0.91R with MFE 1.3–1.56R. The per-tick trail sits
   0.5 ATR off the live extreme; the bar-close trail is strictly looser.
3. **Zero TP exits** (TP = 3 ATR): the per-tick lock+trail truncates the right tail long before 3R.
4. **6/15 shakeouts:** TP printed within 8 bars *after* the exit. Foregone: those 6 banked +1.18R
   vs up to ~18R at TP (upper bound; the validated engine would also have trailed some).

**Important honesty note:** the two full −1R losses (DE40 02:10, SP500 08:45) are
**engine-independent** — the initial 1-ATR stop is identical in both engines and was hit on the
fill bar. The divergence did not cause the losses; it reshapes the *rest* of the distribution.

### P0 fix spec (Option A — make the EA match the validated engine)

Add `InpManageOnBarClose = true` (default) to `DerivScalperEA.mq5`:
- Run lock/trail updates **only when a new M15 bar closes on the position's own symbol**
  (per-symbol bar tracking, NOT the chart symbol's clock — see §4.1).
- Lock condition: **bar-1 close** profit ≥ 0.25·ATR_signal → SL to entry (keep the +spread+2pts
  buffer; it is tiny and conservative in the right direction — document the delta).
- Trail: SL = close₁ ∓ 0.5·ATR_signal, ratchet-only; broker-side SL stays in place between bars
  (execution intrabar on a *static* level matches the harness's "stop tested against the next
  bar's range" semantics).
- **Freeze ATR at signal placement** for lock/trail/TP distances (store per position; stop
  re-reading ATR every tick).
- Anchor the pullback limit to `iClose(sym, tf, 1)` (signal-bar close), not live bid/ask (:384,:392).
- Guard the reload rescan: on init, set `g_lastScanBar = iTime(_Symbol, tf, 0)` so a mid-bar
  reload does not place mid-bar-anchored orders (this happened live at 07:47).
- Align the time exit to **8 closed bars of the position's symbol** (currently seconds-based).
- Keep `ENTRY`/signal/universe/spread-gate code untouched — all validated and behaving.

**Do NOT choose Option B (re-validate per-tick management) as the default path:** M1/M15 data
under-samples a 0.25-ATR intratick trigger, it requires a full walk-forward redo, and the observed
right-tail truncation (0 TPs) would likely fail the gate — meaning the current live engine was
never validated and must change anyway.

**Acceptance (per HANDOFF):** after the change, a demo/live sample's exit mix must be *possible*
under the harness (no moved-stop exits with zero elapsed bar-closes); no change to entry counts;
re-run `walkforward_dsr.py` unchanged (it already models bar-close) to confirm the config is
still the validated one.

- **Per-symbol:** Tech100 +1.36R, DE40 +0.83R†, SmallCap +0.37, WallSt +0.03 vs SP500 −0.97,
  UK100 −0.70†. Meaningless at this N; do not prune symbols on it.
- **Costs & execution:** spread paid ≈ model, fills ≈ limit or better, stop overshoot ≤0.014R,
  churn ~0.02R/trade. **The cost model that carried the SHIP verdict is holding live.** ✅

## 4. Fix list for Cursor (prioritized)

1. **P0 — bar-close management mode** (§1 spec). This is a *fidelity* fix, not a strategy change:
   it makes the live EA the thing that was actually validated. Includes: per-symbol bar clock,
   frozen signal-ATR, signal-close limit anchor, reload guard, bar-based time exit.
2. **P1 — decouple from the chart symbol.** `OnTick` only fires on chart-symbol ticks and the scan
   gate keys off `_Symbol`'s bars (:258). If the chart symbol's market closes, management of open
   positions on other symbols FREEZES between ticks (broker-side SL/TP still active, but locks/
   trails stall). Add `OnTimer` (e.g. 5s) as the management/scan heartbeat, or attach-to-BTCUSD
   as documented interim guidance.
3. **P1 — fix `backtest/live_trade_report.py` R computation.** Convert risk to account currency
   (use `mt5.order_calc_profit` or multiply by the quote-currency rate) — DE40/UK100 R values are
   currently inflated 14%/33%. Also fix the MAE basis (trade #1 shows MAE 1.43R on a 1R stop —
   mixture of the FX artifact and bar-extreme measurement); MAE should use the same denominator
   as R and clip to the exit if the stop was hit intrabar.
4. **P2 — signal + spread logging in the EA (backlog #2).** Log per decision: impulse ATRs,
   ATR, spread/ATR, limit anchor price, and skip reasons. This turns the impulse −1.61 anomaly
   (trade #2) from "probably reconstruction offset" into a checkable fact, and gives the live
   spread-vs-model comparison for free. No behavior change.
5. **P3 — session-gate study on the pullback config (backlog #4)**, motivated by the thin-hours
   bucket. Historical test through the full gate (`experiment.py` marginal + permutation +
   walk-forward). Adopt only on a pass.
6. **No-change items (explicitly):** entry geometry, TP 3.0, momentum gate, universe/spread gate,
   sizing, pending expiry length — all behaving as validated. Do not tune them on 15 trades.

**Guardrail reminder (HANDOFF.md):** none of items 4–5 ship without the marginal-OOS-at-real-cost
gate. Item 1 is exempt from the gate *because it is the reversion to the validated configuration*,
not a new idea — but it must be verified by exit-mix comparison after deployment.

## 5. Bottom line

Day 1 live is **on model financially (+0.96R†, +$5.41) with clean execution and validated costs —
but running an exit engine that was never validated.** The aggregate agreement is luck of a small
sample: 8/15 exits are impossible under the engine that earned the SHIP verdict. Fix P0 before
drawing any further conclusions from live results; until then, live P&L is not evidence about the
validated strategy.
